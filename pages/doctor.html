His Story

A Doctor Called to Serve

Dr. Heuric Rakotomalala was born in Antsirabe, Madagascar and grew up mostly in Antananarivo. From the beginning, he felt called to serve his countrymen — a calling that would take him through years of advanced training before bringing him back to the people who needed him most.

He trained first as a medical doctor in Madagascar, then pursued surgical training through PAACS (Pan African Academy of Christian Surgeons) at Banso Baptist Hospital in Cameroon from 2003, graduating in July 2007. He went on to complete a pediatric surgery fellowship at BethanyKids' Kijabe Hospital in Kenya, graduating in 2010.

After graduation, Dr. Rakotomalala worked one year at BethanyKids as a Junior Consultant Pediatric Surgeon. In March 2011, the Lord brought Heuric and his family back to Madagascar — appointed to work at Antanimalandy Lutheran Hospital in Mahajanga.

"I decide to come back to my home country because there is a terrible lack of pediatric surgeons in the country. There are only 5 pediatric surgeons for 22 million inhabitants."
— Dr. Heuric Rakotomalala, MD

Career Journey

From Madagascar to Africa — and Back

Early Career
Medical Doctor, Madagascar
Completed initial medical training in Madagascar before pursuing advanced surgical specialization.
2003 – 2007
PAACS Surgical Residency — Banso Baptist Hospital, Cameroon
Trained in general surgery through the Pan African Academy of Christian Surgeons. Graduated July 2007.
2007 – 2010
Pediatric Surgery Fellowship — BethanyKids, Kijabe Hospital, Kenya
Completed specialized pediatric surgery training. Graduated 2010. Served briefly as Junior Consultant Pediatric Surgeon at BethanyKids.
2011
Return to Madagascar — Antanimalandy Lutheran Hospital, Mahajanga
Returned home with family. Appointed to Antanimalandy Lutheran Hospital. Also became Chief Medical Officer while training young physicians.
2015 – Present
Medical Director — Manambaro Lutheran Hospital, Southeastern Madagascar
Serving the southeastern region where need for surgeons is "huge." The hospital serves nearly 3 million people with Dr. Rakotomalala as the only certified general and pediatric surgeon. Hosted international medical exchanges with University of Western Michigan.
