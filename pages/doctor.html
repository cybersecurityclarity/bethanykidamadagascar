His Story

A Doctor Called to Serve

Dr. Heuric Rakotomalala was born in Antsirabe, Madagascar and grew up mostly in Antananarivo. From the beginning, he felt called to serve his countrymen — a calling that would take him through years of advanced training before bringing him back to the people who needed him most.

He trained first as a medical doctor in Madagascar, then pursued surgical training through PAACS (Pan African Academy of Christian Surgeons) at Banso Baptist Hospital in Cameroon from 2003, graduating in July 2007. He went on to complete a pediatric surgery fellowship at BethanyKids' Kijabe Hospital in Kenya, graduating in 2010.

After graduation, Dr. Rakotomalala worked one year at BethanyKids as a Junior Consultant Pediatric Surgeon. In March 2011, the Lord brought Heuric and his family back to Madagascar — appointed to work at Antanimalandy Lutheran Hospital in Mahajanga.

[ Photo: Dr. Heuric with team at Manambaro ]
"I decide to come back to my home country because there is a terrible lack of pediatric surgeons in the country. There are only 5 pediatric surgeons for 22 million inhabitants."
— Dr. Heuric Rakotomalala, MD

Impact at Manambaro

Transforming a Region

Since Dr. Rakotomalala began serving at Manambaro Lutheran Hospital in February 2015, the impact on the surrounding region has been immense. Patients no longer need to travel 1,400 km to Antananarivo for pediatric surgical procedures.

Many surrounding hospitals now refer their surgical patients to Dr. Rakotomalala — who sometimes travels over 500 kilometres to reach them. The hospital serves nearly 3 million people.

In addition to his surgical work, Dr. Heuric hosted Professor Dr. Richard Roach from the University of Western Michigan and multiple groups of students for medical exchanges — Malagasy doctors teaching tropical medicine, visiting physicians sharing updates on diabetes, hypertension and stroke treatments.

"Patients no longer have to travel the long distance from southwest Cameroon to the PAACS training centre for surgical procedures that can now be done in Mutengene."
— Parallel story: Dr. Gerald Ekwen, PAACS Alumni Newsletter

Faith & Family

The Whole Person

Dr. Heuric and his wife, Mireille — who is also an internist — have three grown children (Ihoby, Andry and Mirenty), each pursuing their education in the United States.

His daily routine begins with hospital devotions followed by surgical rounds and scheduled procedures. He reflects that PAACS prepared him not just surgically, but spiritually: "PAACS helped me to live a daily life honoring God — performing high quality surgery, handling difficult situations with learned skills, and having the Great Healer beside me."

"God is truly our Provider since the beginning," he states. Dr. Heuric is committed to training future surgeons at Manambaro Lutheran Hospital and across Madagascar through PAACS.
